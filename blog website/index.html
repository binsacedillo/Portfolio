FOCUS: Japan's G-7 leadership role faces tests at decarbonization talks

As Japan prepares to host the Group of Seven summit, doubts have arisen about their ability to lead climate change discussions due to their continued use of fossil fuels. Despite claims of success by Environment Minister Akihiro Nishimura in laying out specific steps to address climate change, Japan's reliance on coal has prevented the group from setting a deadline for phasing out power plants fired by the polluting energy source. The G-7 ministers have retained the commitment to achieving a "fully or predominantly" decarbonized power sector by 2035, but some countries have called for removing the word "predominantly."
